What is the one critical decision in your system that must remain human?
Since Fast Bundle is a non-consumer-facing productivity product, I don't think there is any inherent need to maintain a human connection with customers. So, the long-term vision is for this system to accurately determine issues and even perform troubleshooting without any human involvement. However, the limitations of the current LLM models make it crucial to keep a human in the loop. More specifically, at this stage of development, members of Fast Bundle's support or key account management team must manually review all detected failures and try to resolve the issues. More importantly, this step is necessary to provide feedback, as there may be many false positives. Their feedback will lead to adding more deterministic checks and more nuanced prompts for the LLM.
