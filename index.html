What problem is this system solving?

This platform automates health checks for Fast Bundle across Shopify stores.

Fast Bundle adds merchandising and discount functionality to storefronts, but every Shopify store is customized differently. Because of theme changes, app conflicts, discount logic changes, or store-specific implementations, Fast Bundle’s functionality in a store can break.

Previously, this was handled through manual health checks for enterprise users (the highest-tier plan)—and that initiative dramatically improved net revenue retention for that segment. But the approach still had three limitations:

  • It was becoming harder to scale as the number of enterprise merchants grew.
  • It could only be offered to a limited segment of customers, mainly higher-tier plans, rather than across the broader customer base.
  • It was periodic rather than immediate, so issues could remain undetected between checks.

The platform solves this by automatically testing whether Fast Bundle is installed, visible, usable, and applying discounts correctly, then logging the results for follow-up.

So, in short, what’s the benefit of this system?

The main benefit is that bundle health checks can now be automated across the entire customer base, not just for enterprise merchants, and can be performed both when a bundle is created and on an ongoing periodic basis.

That creates value in three ways:

  • It expands coverage, making proactive monitoring possible for all customers.
  • It can further improve net revenue retention, by catching issues earlier and maintaining a more reliable merchant experience.
  • It frees up Fast Bundle’s support and key account management teams to spend less time on repetitive functionality checks and more time helping merchants with higher-value problems, especially optimizing bundling strategy and driving better results from the app.
What is the one critical decision in this system that must remain human?

The critical decision that must remain human is the final judgment on whether a detected failure is truly a customer-impacting issue and what action should be taken next.

My long-term vision is for the system to diagnose issues very accurately and eventually handle a large portion of troubleshooting automatically. But today, LLMs are still imperfect, especially in messy real-world storefront environments with pop-ups, custom themes, and ambiguous UI states.

Because of that, a human from support or key account management still needs to:

  • review failures flagged by the system,
  • identify false positives,
  • decide whether intervention is necessary,
  • and provide feedback that improves the system over time.

That human review step is not just a safeguard; it is also what creates the feedback loop needed to make the platform more reliable through better prompts, more deterministic checks, and better categorization logic.

What would break first at scale?

The first thing that would break at scale is accuracy across edge cases.

Shopify stores vary widely in theme structure, UI behavior, and discount logic, so as volume grows, the system would likely face more false positives and false negatives. If that happens, the human review layer could become the new bottleneck.

So the main scaling challenge is not whether the system can run more checks, but whether it can stay reliable enough that the output remains useful. Over time, the solution would be to turn repeated patterns into deterministic checks and reserve LLM reasoning for the more ambiguous cases.
